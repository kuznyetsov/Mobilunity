Challenges

Migrating to new technologies

As a mature product, Xpolog Log Analysis Platform faced the need of upgrading UI and migrating it to a newer technology that would:

foster positive user
experience

ensure application withstanding
the increasing data load

allow for higher speed
of response

Enhancing scalability

Xpolog needed to select the technologies that will qualitatively advance the speed of development and ensure that no matter how many new developers will join the team in the future, the onboarding would be prompt and easy.

The product was insufficiently documented at the early stage of its development, and now to support the complex platform it grew to become, the team had to produce and accumulate thorough developer’s manuals.

Improve communication within the team

Xpolog has tried outsourcing teams before, but it seemed they had to deal with PMs more than developers. This has lead to frequent miscommunications and time wasted on transferring tasks from team to team. They needed to find a better way to build distributed teams.

Solution

Xpolog requested Mobilunity to build a dedicated team of skilful frontend developers and undertake migrating the frontend part of the application to latest Angular.

Project highlights

The Mobilunity’s dedicated team model provided a direct contact between the Xpolog’s in-house backend and Mobilunity’s frontend teams without any limits on format or frequency of communication, which boosted efficiency significantly.

The decision was made to migrate the project’s frontend to newest Angular and use Redux to organize the code, which resulted in growing page rendering speed and advancing cross-platform compatibility.

Introduce changes step by step made it possible to preserve old features crucial for the customers and introduce new technologies gradually, switching to a more usable UI design, boosting performance and building the solid foundation for extending the product’s capabilities in the future.

As Xpolog is a well-positioned company with dozens of enterprise level customers and an extremely complex product, it was chosen to introduce changes step by step, replacing Java with Angular page by page.

This approach made it possible to preserve old features crucial for the customers and introduce new technologies gradually switching to a more usable UI design, boosting performance and building the solid foundation for extending the product’s capabilities in the future.

Another important step was adding a QA engineer to the Mobilunity part of the team to create and maintain the knowledge database that contains all information about Xpolog application development.

Current Outcome

WIth Mobiliunity’s model of teams augmentation, Xpolog has successfully extended their developers’ crew with high-performing programmers based in Ukraine.

The dedicated team model and support from Mobilunity’s side helped Xpolog leverage communication within the joint team and ensure faster and better results.

The migration is currently in progress, but noticeable acceleration of the development and positive feedback received from Xpolog’s customers prove that the efforts are put in the right direction.

Knowledge database is growing to be an efficient tool to keep track of the technical approaches as well as the business logic behind the product features and this will eventually facilitate product’s development in the upcoming years.
